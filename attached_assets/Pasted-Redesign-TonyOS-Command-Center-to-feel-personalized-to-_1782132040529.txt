Redesign TonyOS Command Center to feel personalized to Tony Casella while staying executive, premium, and founder-level.

TonyOS should not feel like a generic dark dashboard. It should feel like Tony’s private executive command center.

Tony is:
- Founder-level strategic partner
- Contractor
- Engineer
- Practical and smart
- Genuinely good person
- Fun and personable
- Likes fishing
- Likes football
- Likes travel
- Likes working out / gym
- Likes the ocean

Do not make this cheesy or themed like a hobby app. Do not add cartoon fish, football wallpaper, gym graphics, or travel clipart.

Use Tony’s personality as subtle design language:
- Ocean depth
- Compass/navigation
- Field strategy
- Engineering precision
- Contractor practicality
- Strength/capacity
- Travel/expansion map
- Warm leadership tone

Visual direction:
- Dark graphite executive shell
- Charcoal and storm-gray panels
- Deep ocean/navy-black background
- Ocean teal and sea-glass accents
- Electric blue only for important command actions
- Silver/steel borders and engineering lines
- Subtle blueprint grid or technical-line details
- Premium boardroom intelligence console
- Clean, strong, grounded, not overly blue

Avoid:
- Heavy blue dashboard
- White admin cards
- Pink
- Purple
- Gold
- Orange
- Beige
- Fishing/football/gym/travel gimmicks
- Cartoon graphics
- Decorative blobs or orbs
- Generic SaaS look

Rename some sections to feel more Tony-specific:
- Executive Home -> TonyOS Home
- Strategic Pulse -> Strategic Compass
- Financial Review -> Founder Financials
- Major Decisions -> Game Plan
- Risk Platform -> Risk Tide
- Predictors -> Forward Signals
- Brand Portfolio -> Expansion Map
- Company Brain -> Company Intelligence

Dashboard modules:
1. TonyOS Command Center
   Subtitle: CAG oversight, founder strategy, and forward signals.

2. Strategic Compass
   Parent-company direction, growth path, brand priorities, expansion signals.

3. Expansion Map
   CAG parent company down to CCA and future brands.

4. Game Plan
   Major decisions, founder approvals, high-priority strategic moves.

5. Founder Financials
   Revenue, reserve runway, cash timing, debt exposure, payroll coverage.

6. Risk Tide
   Client risk, fulfillment risk, legal/CLP risk, system risk, brand risk.

7. Forward Signals
   Predictors for revenue, client risk, capacity, knowledge gaps, duplicate work, and strategic opportunities.

8. On Deck
   Top items that need Tony’s review this week.

9. Deep Dive
   Drill-down area for CCA, subsidiaries, projects, decisions, and source records.

10. Tony Mode
   A small personal panel with subtle icons or labels:
   - Ocean
   - Travel
   - Game Day
   - Strength
   - Field Notes

Keep Tony Mode small and tasteful. It should make the dashboard feel personal without weakening the executive feel.

Authority guardrails:
Tony has founder-level visibility and strategic participation, but the system must not imply automatic day-to-day operational control, bank signing authority, unilateral spending authority, payroll authority, hiring/firing authority, debt authority, or authority to bind the company.

Success test:
If the page feels like a generic dark dashboard, it failed.
If it feels like a polished executive command center that only Tony would have, it passed.
