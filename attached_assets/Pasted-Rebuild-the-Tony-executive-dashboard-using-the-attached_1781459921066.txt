Rebuild the Tony executive dashboard using the attached TonyOS Command Center mockup as the required visual design reference.

This is not optional inspiration. Match the design direction closely.

Product name:
TonyOS Command Center

Purpose:
TonyOS is the founder-level executive command center for Tony Casella. It gives him strategic oversight, financial visibility, major decision review, risk/platform strategy visibility, and drill-down access without making him a day-to-day operator.

Required visual direction:
- Dark navy executive app shell
- White work surfaces
- Electric blue primary accents
- Steel/silver dividers and borders
- Graphite/slate secondary surfaces
- Restrained green, amber, and red status chips only
- Premium B2B SaaS / executive command center feel
- Clean, serious, strategic, investor-grade
- Compact but not crowded
- Strong hierarchy
- Crisp typography
- Rounded corners should stay subtle, around 6px max

Do NOT use:
- Pink
- Purple
- Lavender
- Magenta
- Rose-colored gradients
- Yellow-heavy Rose Brain styling
- Orange construction themes
- Generic colorful SaaS gradients
- Decorative orbs
- Bokeh blobs
- Cartoon/illustration styling
- Marketing landing page layout

Required layout:
1. Left sidebar
   - Top identity: TonyOS
   - Smaller label: Command Center
   - Navigation:
     - Executive Home
     - Strategic Pulse
     - Financial Review
     - Major Decisions
     - Risk Platform
     - Company Brain

2. Top bar
   - Breadcrumb: Rose OS / TonyOS Command Center
   - User chip: Tony Casella - Founder-Level Strategic Oversight

3. Main page title
   - TonyOS Command Center
   - Subtitle: Founder-level strategy, financial visibility, and major decision oversight

4. Top KPI row
   Include four executive KPI tiles:
   - Company Pulse
   - Collected Retained Revenue
   - Operating Reserve
   - Major Decisions Pending

5. Executive Brief panel
   - Title: Executive Brief
   - Subtitle or label: Top 5 Things Tony Should Know
   - Each row should have a short summary, status chip, and drill-down arrow

6. Major Decision Queue panel
   Include approval-needed items with status chips:
   - Written Approval
   - Budget Threshold
   - IP / Platform Rights
   - Financial Commitment

7. Risk & Platform Strategy panel
   Include four cards:
   - Compliance Risk Audit
   - Insurance Readiness
   - Bonding Readiness
   - AI / Platform Roadmap

8. Monthly Founder Financial Review panel
   Include:
   - Mini chart
   - Revenue trigger progress
   - Reserve target
   - Payroll
   - Debt
   - Accounts receivable summary

9. Governance Guardrails panel
   Include read-only indicator chips:
   - Strategic Review
   - Financial Visibility
   - No Unilateral Bank Authority
   - No Day-to-Day Operations

10. CLP separation alert
   Add a small understated alert strip:
   “CLP separation guardrail: approved company systems and cleared source records only.”

Functional behavior:
- Every KPI tile should be clickable or clearly prepared for drill-down.
- Executive Brief rows should drill into related project, decision, financial, or Company Brain records.
- Major Decision Queue should be review/approval-oriented, not task-board oriented.
- Financial Review should be visibility-only unless a separate written approval workflow exists.
- TonyOS should not expose admin/system write controls by default.
- TonyOS should not give Tony unilateral bank, spending, payroll, hiring/firing, debt, or company-binding authority.

Agreement-based guardrails:
Tony’s role is founder-level strategic oversight, not day-to-day operational management. Build the UI around strategy, visibility, governance, and major decision review.

Final requirement:
Before marking complete, compare the app visually against the attached TonyOS Command Center mockup. If the screen uses pink, purple, lavender, magenta, yellow-heavy branding, orange themes, or generic gradients, it is not complete.
